# Math Island Intro Design

## Style Prompt
Create a warm kids-game trailer for a Grade 1 math island adventure. The world should feel sunny, tactile, and friendly: a painted island map, big readable type, soft paper-like panels, simple math objects, star rewards, and playful motion. The mood is energetic but never noisy, with clear educational moments that a 5-6 year-old can understand at a glance.

## Colors
- Sky ocean background: `#7FD4EC`
- Deep readable text: `#184E5A`
- Island grass: `#7CCB66`
- Warm sand panels: `#FFE8A8`
- Coral action accent: `#FF7A6B`
- Star reward yellow: `#FFD84D`

## Typography
- Display: `Baloo 2`, 800-900, large and rounded for kid-facing headlines.
- Support: `Fraunces`, 700 for labels and small emphasis.
- Body/UI: `Atkinson Hyperlegible`, 500-700 for readability.

## Motion
- Entrances bounce or float in with short staggered timing.
- Transitions use warm light wipes and circle reveals.
- Objects move on simple arcs so the math action is legible.

## What NOT to Do
- Do not use dark tech palettes, neon gradients, or purple-blue product styling.
- Do not crowd the frame with long sentences.
- Do not use worksheet-like layouts as the main visual.
- Do not make the map tiny; the island must be a first-frame signal.
