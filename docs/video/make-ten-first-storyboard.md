# Math Island video storyboard: Make 10 First

Project: Math Island kids explainer
Audience: ages 5-6
Core problem: 8 + 5
Core strategy: make 10 first
Target runtime: 38 seconds
Narration file: /Users/mac/.hermes/audio_cache/tts_20260601_095348.mp3

## Teaching goal
By the end of the video, a child should understand that 8 + 5 can be solved by splitting 5 into 2 and 3, making 10 first, and then adding the leftover 3.

## Visual style
- Warm island sky and sandy beach
- Friendly shell counters
- Large numerals and short caption phrases
- One visual motion per beat
- Equation appears after the object idea is clear

## Scene 1 — Hook
Time: 0.0s to 5.0s
Narration:
"Hmm... eight plus five. That feels a little tricky. But watch this."
Visuals:
- Title card: "Math Island"
- Large equation: 8 + 5 = ?
- 8 shells on the left, 5 shells on the right
Teaching beat:
- This problem may feel hard at first, and that is okay.

## Scene 2 — Eight is close to ten
Time: 5.0s to 10.0s
Narration:
"Eight only needs two more to make ten."
Visuals:
- Left group of 8 shells highlighted
- Two empty glowing spots appear to complete a row of 10
- Caption: "8 needs 2 more"
Teaching beat:
- 8 is near 10, so 10 is the helpful target.

## Scene 3 — Split the 5
Time: 10.0s to 15.0s
Narration:
"So we can split five into two and three."
Visuals:
- Right group of 5 shells separates into a pair and a trio
- Pair glows teal, trio stays coral
- Caption: "5 = 2 + 3"
Teaching beat:
- Numbers can break apart without changing the total.

## Scene 4 — Move the 2 and make 10
Time: 15.0s to 21.0s
Narration:
"Move the two over. Now we made ten first."
Visuals:
- The glowing pair travels from the right group to the left group
- Left side locks into a golden row of 10 shells
- The 3-shell leftover remains visible on the right
- Caption: "Make 10 first"
- Equation transition: 8 + 5 -> 8 + 2 + 3
Teaching beat:
- The strategy is to make 10 before finishing the addition.

## Scene 5 — Solve it
Time: 21.0s to 26.0s
Narration:
"Ten and three make thirteen. So eight plus five equals thirteen."
Visuals:
- Golden 10 shells plus coral 3 shells
- Equation transition: 10 + 3 = 13
- Final answer badge: 13
Teaching beat:
- Once 10 is made, the answer becomes easy to finish.

## Scene 6 — Try the trick again
Time: 26.0s to 34.0s
Narration:
"Try it again. Nine plus six becomes ten plus five. Seven plus eight becomes ten plus five."
Visuals:
- Split screen mini-example A: 9 + 6 -> 10 + 5 -> 15
- Split screen mini-example B: 7 + 8 -> 10 + 5 -> 15
- Repeated banner: "Make 10 first"
Teaching beat:
- This is a reusable strategy, not just one answer.

## Scene 7 — Your turn
Time: 34.0s to 37.8s
Narration:
"Your turn: eight plus six. How many do you move to make ten first?"
Visuals:
- Large challenge card: 8 + 6 = ?
- 8 shells shown with 6 shells waiting
- Gentle pause with a blinking question bubble
Teaching beat:
- Invite the child to apply the strategy independently.

## End state
- The child has seen the problem, the split, the move, the new easy equation, and two transfer examples.
- Final frame should hold long enough for a parent or child to answer the challenge aloud.
